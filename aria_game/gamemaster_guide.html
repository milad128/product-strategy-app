GM GUIDE

PROTOCOL: OVERRIDE · Step-by-step playbook

This game runs on two screens: the projector shows the game page (/game). You run the Game Master panel on your laptop (/gamemaster).

Players advance most steps on the projector. Your job is to configure the session, start the game, monitor teams and scores, run the mission timer, and intervene when needed.

All progress is saved in aria_game.db. Use Reboot or Reset act only when you mean to clear data.

PREP Before the game Control panel · configuration

What happens

The server is idle. The projector shows a white screen with START. No teams or scores exist yet (unless you resumed a saved session).

What you should do

  1. Start the server: python aria_game/server.py
  2. Open the projector on /game (full screen).
  3. Open /gamemaster on your laptop.
  4. In General configuration, set:
    • Mission timer (minutes) — countdown shown in Step 3.
    • Mission names and passphrases for all 5 missions.
  5. In Step configuration, set Persian ARIA text for Step 1 and Step 2, and the Step 2 registration timer (seconds).
  6. Click Save on each section after editing.
  7. Open /gamemaster/routes and keep the secret URLs + passphrases for your physical puzzles. Do not show this to players.
  8. If testing earlier, click Reboot to clear teams and scores before guests arrive.
STEP 0 Start Projector · white screen

What happens

The projector displays a minimal white page with a START button. Nothing else runs yet.

What you should do

Your action Click Start in the control panel — or let a player click START on the projector. Either starts Step 1.

The control panel Start button is only enabled while the game is idle (Step 0).

STEP 1 ARIA intro Projector · Matrix theme

What happens

  • Matrix-style visuals appear on the projector.
  • ARIA speaks the Step 1 Persian narration (neural TTS).
  • Text types on screen. An Accept & continue button appears when ready.

What you should do

Your action Let the narration finish. You can edit Step 1 text beforehand in the control panel if needed. When the room is ready, have someone click Accept & continue on the projector to move to Step 2.
You do not click anything on the control panel to leave Step 1 — advancement is triggered from the game screen.
STEP 2 Team building Projector · registration form

What happens

  • ARIA plays the Step 2 Persian narration.
  • A registration countdown runs (duration from Step 2 config).
  • Players enter 4 team names and up to 6 members per team on the projector.
  • If they register in time: teams and members are saved exactly as entered.
  • If time expires: an alarm plays, the button becomes Enter the teams, and members are shuffled across the named teams (max 6 per team). ARIA plays a Persian “you failed to collaborate” line.
  • Registered teams appear on the control panel under Teams & scores.

What you should do

Your action
  1. Encourage squads to agree on names and enter members before the timer ends.
  2. Watch the control panel to confirm all four teams registered.
  3. After teams are shown, have someone click Continue to mission on the projector to start Step 3.
If registration goes wrong, use Reset act (keeps teams, clears scores, restarts current step) or Reboot (full wipe except your saved config).
STEP 3 Missions Projector · timer · leaderboard · mission board

What happens

  • Mission timer counts down at the top (duration from General configuration).
  • Protocol phrase shows blank word slots; each word reveals when any team completes that mission.
  • Leaderboard ranks squads by score and missions cleared.
  • Mission board lists all 5 missions with status: ARMED → ACTIVE → BREACHED.
  • Squads must discover each mission’s secret URL and passphrase from in-room puzzles, then POST: { "squad": "TEAMNAME", "passphrase": "WORD" }
  • First squad to breach a mission earns the most points (3 / 2 / 1 / 0 by rank).
  • When a squad clears all 5 layers, the game ends and a winner is declared.

What you should do

Your action
  1. Start the mission timer when gameplay begins: curl -X POST http://localhost:8765/gm/timer/start (use your laptop’s LAN IP if running curl from another machine).
  2. Give squads physical clues that lead to each route on the routes cheat sheet — never put the cheat sheet on the projector.
  3. Monitor the leaderboard and mission board on the projector.
  4. Pause or resume the timer if needed: /gm/timer/pause · /gm/timer/resume
  5. Adjust scores for bonuses or penalties: /gm/score/adjust with squad, delta, reason
  6. Push dramatic messages to the transmission log: /gm/transmission
Squad names on the API must match the team names registered in Step 2 (case-insensitive).
REF GM tools reference Control panel buttons

Start

Step 0 → Step 1. Same as projector START. Disabled after the game has begun.

Reset act

Clears mission scores and progress. Stays on the current step. Keeps registered teams. Restarts Step 2 timer if you are on team building.

Reboot

Full reset to Step 0. Clears teams, scores, and leaderboard. Keeps all GM configuration (steps + missions).

Save configs

General + Step 1 + Step 2 settings persist in SQLite. Edit anytime; mission timer duration updates before the timer is started.

Routes cheat sheet

Secret POST paths and passphrases at /gamemaster/routes. For GM eyes only.

Teams & scores

Live view of registered squads, members, per-act ranks, and total points.
